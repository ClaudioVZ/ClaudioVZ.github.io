Eliminación hacia adelante revisitado

Los cálculos innecesarios se eliminarán para aumentar la velocidad $$ \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{llll} a_{11} = a_{11} & a_{12} = a_{12} & a_{13} = a_{13} & a_{14} = a_{14} \\ & a_{22} = a_{22}' & a_{23} = a_{23}' & a_{24} = a_{24}' \\ & & a_{33} = a_{33}'' & a_{34} = a_{34}'' \\ & & & a_{44} = a_{44}''' \end{array} \end{equation*} $$

Fórmula matemática

$$ \begin{align*} k &= 1, \dots, m - 1 \\ & \quad i = 1 + k, \dots, m \\ & \quad \quad j = 1 + k, \dots, n \\ & \quad \quad \quad a_{ij} = a_{ij} - \frac{a_{ik}}{a_{kk}} a_{kj} \end{align*} $$

Seudocódigo 

function eliminacion_adelante(a)
    m, n = tamaño(a)
    for k=1 to m-1 do
        for i=1+k to m do
            for j=1+k to n do
                a(i,j) = a(i,j) - a(i,k)*a(k,j)/a(k,k)
            end for
        end for
    end for
end function

otra alternativa para reducir tiempo de cálculo 

function eliminacion_adelante(a)
    m, n = tamaño(a)
    for k=1 to m-1 do
        for i=1+k to m do
            factor = a(i,k)/a(k,k)
            for j=1+k to n do
                a(i,j) = a(i,j) - factor*a(k,j)
            end for
        end for
    end for
end function

Implementación eliminación hacia adelante 

import numpy as np

def eliminacion_adelante(A):
    a = np.copy(A)
    m, n = a.shape
    for k in range(0,m-1):
        for i in range(1+k,m):
            factor = a[i,k]/a[k,k]
            for j in range(1+k,n):
                a[i,j] = a[i,j] - factor*a[k,j]
    return a


A = np.array([[3,-0.1,-0.2],[0.1,7,-0.3],[0.3,-0.2,10]])
print(A)


    [[  3.   -0.1  -0.2]

B = eliminacion_adelante(A)
